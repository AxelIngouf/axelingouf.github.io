PROJECTS

The Loony Bin, Unreal, 2021

The game I'm the most proud of yet. The Loony Bin is part of a 4 days hackathon project, the Isart Game Week 2021 with a team of 2 programmers, 3 3D artists, 2 game designers and 1 sound designer. Our goal was to make a game on the theme of "Generation". So we came up with this horror game taking place in a randomly generated maze inspired by Amnesia and SCP-087.

I mainly worked on the maze generation, with the goal of having a playable level each time you play with all sorts of randomness in the items, rooms, lights and decorative objects placement.

The other programmer, Valentin Dol, worked on other aspects of the game, like the light/shadow design, collectibles and even the enemy. Most of the 3D and 2D assets were made by our 2 main 3D artists, Anna Le Cossec and Juliette Sirulnick. They were very professionals and I had a lot of fun working with them for a second time. Bomberman, Java, 2018
First real game I did outside my courses. At the time I wanted to make a game I could play with my friends on the school computers, which we eventually did during way to much lunch breaks.

The first version was made using an old 2D java game engine made by previous students of my school. And the second version was made using SDL library in C++, because I wanted to train before applying to Isart Digital Montréal.

Every character can be controlled either by a human or by an AI. For the AI, I used a A* search algorithm, and it will choose if it goes for the kill or if something more interesting is near. Obviously, the part where it kills itself was not intended.
